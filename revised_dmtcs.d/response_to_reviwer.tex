\bigskip\bigskip
 
\noindent
We thank the reviewer for reading the manuscript carefully and
providing very helpful suggestions.
We have incorporated all the corrections under the section entitled
``Minor" in the review.
Below, we discuss how the two items mentioned under the section
entitled ``Major"  in the review
have been addressed in the revised version.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The statement of Part (ii) of Corollary 3.4 has been corrected. 
It now reads as follows: 
``An $r$-symmetric NCF has a default-normalized
nested canalyzing representation with at most $r$ layers."

\item We have changed the statement of Theorem 3.6 to point out that
the algorithm runs in time that is linear in the \emph{size of the input}.
After the statement of the theorem, we have pointed out that when
$r$ is fixed, the size of the input and the running time of the 
algorithm are both polynomial functions of $n$, 
the number of variables.
If $r$ depends on $n$, then the input size and the running time
of the algorithm are not polynomial functions of $n$.
\end{enumerate}
